MANY KINDS OF HEALTHY TEA

Hibiscus Tea

Hibiscus tea: Research in animals, test tubes, and preliminary studies suggests that hibiscus may aid in weight loss, improve heart and liver health, and even reduce the growth of cancer cells and bacteria.

Butterfly Tea

Blue butterfly pea flower tea: It helps regulate blood glucose levels, making it particularly beneficial for controlling diabetes. The saponins and flavonoids present in butterfly pea play a key role in preventing asthma. It also contains anthocyanin, which helps block the production of free radicals in the body, thereby aiding in cancer prevention.

Ginger Tea

Ginger tea: It can be consumed as a remedy for nausea, digestive issues, and cold symptoms. Some studies also suggest that it may help regulate blood glucose levels and be beneficial for managing non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD). Additionally, its anti-inflammatory properties can assist individuals suffering from arthritis.

Lemon Tea

Lemon tea: This popular anti-inflammatory breakfast drink works wonders by boosting your immune system, speeding up metabolism, and regulating the digestive system. Due to the high citric acid content in lemons, it also helps flush out toxins from the liver.
